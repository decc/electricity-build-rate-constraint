Low carbon electricity ready reckoner

Tries to show a scenario for the electricity sector that has 2050 emissions from electricity below 10 MtCO2 in 2050 given the following assumptions. Please note it is ALPHA and is currently not to be trusted.

Warning: cannot get 2050 electricity emissions below 10 MtCO2 given these assumptions. Try increasing the maximum low carbon build rate after 2020.

Inputs relating to 2012-2020

Low carbon build rate from now to 2020: GW/y
Proportion of this build rate that is wind: of build
Non-renewable low carbon generation in 2020 (i.e., nuclear, CCS) TWh/y
Emissions factor for fossil fuel generation in 2020: gCO2/kWh

Inputs relating to 2020-2050

Target low carbon build rate: GW/y
Proportion of this build rate that is wind: of build
Year in which widespread electrification of demand starts:
Electricity demand in 2050: TWh/y
Maximum electricity demand as a proportion of the demand: 100% implies perfect energy storage. 200% means peak demand is twice mean demand'
Minimum electricity demand as a proportion of the mean demand: 100% implies perfect energy storage. 0% means electricity demand sometimes falls to zero.
Emissions factor for fossil fuel generation in 2050: gCO2/kWh

Inputs relating to whole period

Annual change in non-electricity traded emissions: These are mainly industrial emissions.
Average lifespan of intermittent low carbon generation (i.e., wind): Years
Average lifespan of other low carbon generation: Years
Average lifespan of high carbon generation: Years
Maximum drop in low carbon construction rate in a year: 0% = can't shrink, 50% = can halve in a year, 100% = can drop to zero in a year
Maximum increase in low carbon construction rate in a year: Proportion of previous year's construction (0 means can't expand, 50% means can grow by half each year, 100% means can double in size each year)
Level of low carbon construction capacity that will always be available: GW/y (even if the industry has been completely collapsed, could still get this much in a year though imports or restarting the industr)

This is based on a very simple one sheet excel model.Please download source, report bugs and propose changes at http://github.com/decc/electricity-build-rate-constraint.
